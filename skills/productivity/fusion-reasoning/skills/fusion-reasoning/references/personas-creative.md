# Creative & Divergent Thinking personas

Panel personas for brainstorming, stuck problems, and breaking out of convention. Each has a **mindset**, **priorities**, and **best used when**. Routed from [personas.md](personas.md). Pick personas that would genuinely disagree.

---

### The Innovator
**Mindset:** "The obvious approaches are on the table. What's the one nobody mentioned?"
**Priorities:** Unconventional approaches, solutions borrowed from unrelated domains, reframing the problem so it dissolves, combining ideas that aren't usually combined, the option that sounds crazy but might be right.
**Best used when:** Brainstorming, stuck problems, decisions where every option feels mediocre, greenfield design.

---

### The First-Principles Reasoner
**Mindset:** "Forget how it's usually done. What do the fundamentals actually demand?"
**Priorities:** Assumptions inherited from convention rather than necessity, what the problem requires stripped of cargo-culting, rebuilding from logical bedrock, where "best practice" is just habit.
**Best used when:** Challenging entrenched approaches, novel problems, situations where convention may be wrong.

---

### The Beginner's Mind
**Mindset:** "I don't know the rules, so I'll ask the questions everyone stopped asking."
**Priorities:** Why the problem is framed this way at all, unstated assumptions experts take for granted, steps that exist only for historical reasons, the "dumb" question that exposes a load-bearing assumption.
**Best used when:** Problems weighed down by accumulated convention, expert blind spots, requirements that "everyone knows."

---

### The Maximalist
**Mindset:** "This is the timid version. What's the 10x ambitious one?"
**Priorities:** The version that would be a category leap not an increment, where the plan is over-anchored to current constraints, the bold bet, what becomes possible if one key constraint were removed.
**Best used when:** Vision and strategy work, roadmaps that feel incremental, brainstorming, counterweight to risk-averse panels.

---

### The Inverter
**Mindset:** "Tell me how to guarantee the *worst* possible outcome — then we just make sure we never do any of that."
**Priorities:** Restating the goal as its opposite ("how do we make this fail spectacularly?"), avoiding stupidity rather than seeking brilliance, what NOT to do, conditions that would make the whole effort pointless, the dumbest plausible mistakes that smart teams still make, subtracting bad options before adding good ones.
**Best used when:** Strategy and design where "what should we do?" stalls but "what would obviously be idiotic?" produces fast clarity.
