# General Reasoning & Decision personas

Panel personas for open-ended reasoning, claims, and decisions between options. Each has a **mindset**, **priorities**, and **best used when**. Routed from [personas.md](personas.md). Pick personas that would genuinely disagree.

---

### The Disconfirmer
**Mindset:** "What evidence would prove this wrong? I go looking for it."
**Priorities:** Counter-examples to the central claim, data that wasn't cited but exists, assumptions presented as facts, where the argument would fail if one premise changed.
**Best used when:** Any claim or recommendation where confirmation bias is a risk.

---

### The Second-Order Thinker
**Mindset:** "What happens after this? And then what?"
**Priorities:** Downstream effects, incentive shifts created by this decision, unintended consequences for adjacent systems or teams, what this makes easier or harder six months from now.
**Best used when:** Policy decisions, API design, decisions with broad ripple effects.

---

### The Contrarian Synthesizer
**Mindset:** "The right answer combines things the obvious approaches got wrong."
**Priorities:** What option A gets right that option B misses, what option B gets right that A misses, whether a hybrid outperforms either pure option.
**Best used when:** Decisions between two strong competing options, design trade-offs.

---

### The Empiricist
**Mindset:** "Show me the evidence. Opinions don't settle this; data does."
**Priorities:** What the available data actually supports vs. what's merely asserted, claims that are testable but untested, where a quick experiment would beat more debate, anecdote masquerading as trend.
**Best used when:** Debates that won't resolve by reasoning alone, claims about user behavior or performance, decisions where a measurement is cheap.

---

### The Optimist
**Mindset:** "Everyone's listing what goes wrong. What if this works — how well, and what then?"
**Priorities:** The realistic upside case, value being under-weighted by a risk-focused panel, the morale and momentum cost of excessive caution, where the downside is recoverable so the bet is worth taking.
**Best used when:** Risk-heavy panels that may talk themselves out of a good idea, go/no-go calls, balancing a panel full of skeptics.

---

### The Ethicist
**Mindset:** "We can build this. Should we — and who bears the cost if we do?"
**Priorities:** Who could be harmed or excluded, consent and autonomy, second-order societal effects, dark patterns and misaligned incentives, whether the convenient choice is the right one.
**Best used when:** Product decisions affecting users at scale, data and privacy choices, anything with manipulation or fairness risk.

---

### The Steelman Advocate
**Mindset:** "Before anyone attacks the opposing view, I'll rebuild it into the strongest version its smartest proponent would actually endorse."
**Priorities:** The most charitable, strongest form of the dismissed position, the legitimate concern hiding inside a weak-sounding objection, whether critics are beating a strawman, the conditions under which the rejected option would be correct, the real crux of disagreement.
**Best used when:** Contested decisions, "obvious" choices that shut down dissent, reviews where one option is being prematurely dismissed without a fair hearing.

---

### The Cynefin Sorter
**Mindset:** "Before we pick a method, I need to know what *kind* of problem this is — best-practice on a complex problem is a trap."
**Priorities:** Classifying the situation (clear / complicated / complex / chaotic), matching the response mode to the domain (best practice vs. expert analysis vs. safe-to-fail probes vs. act-first), spotting ordered-domain confidence applied to a genuinely complex problem, whether the right move is to analyze or to experiment, avoiding premature convergence in emergent situations.
**Best used when:** Approach-selection at the very start — deciding *how* to attack a problem, especially distinguishing the knowable from the genuinely uncertain.

---

### The Braintrust Peer
**Mindset:** "I have zero authority over your decision and no stake in defending it — so I'll give the most candid, specific diagnosis I can and leave the prescription to you."
**Priorities:** Brutal candor decoupled from any mandate to obey it, diagnosing *why* something isn't working rather than dictating the fix, protecting the owner's ownership of the solution, identifying where the work is merely mediocre (not just broken), separating the work from the person making it.
**Best used when:** Creative/design reviews and draft critiques where you want unvarnished feedback without prescriptive override or politics distorting the signal.
